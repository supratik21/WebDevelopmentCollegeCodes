Whitehaven Beach Queensland

History:

The beach was named and discovered in 1879 by Staff Commander EP Bedwell. 'Whitehaven' specifically originates from the English town of the same name, it was one of the many names, from the then English county of Cumberland, which Bedwell brought to the area. It followed James Cook's 1770 naming of the island group, the Cumberland Islands.

Landmarks:

  • Whitsunday Islands National Park
  • Hamilton Island
  • Tongue Bay
  • Beautiful white sand and crystal clear blue water
  • Perfect for a cruise and enjoy a delicious morning tea on board
  • Pleasant lookout walk just behind the beach

Wineglass Bay Tasmania

History:

The name Wineglass bay comes from the very famous practice that is done on this coast. The name is derived from the whale oil industry that was being practiced on the beach. The industry would often butcher the whales at the beach.

Twilight Beach Western Australia

History:

The cove has been the location of a number of shipwrecks. The Twilight (after which it is named) and the Bunyip were swept ashore during a storm on 24 May 1877. Both had been involved in transporting materials for the construction of a telegraph line. On 31 August 1896, the Swift struck a rock and was wrecked.

Landmarks:

  • Pink Lake
  • Espearance Beach
  • Espearance Golf Course
  • The beach is lovely - pure white sand and beautiful clear water
  • Lots of nice vegetation fringing the beach area
  • Enjoy snorkelling on the East side
Hyams Beach New South Wales

History:

Hyam's Beach is named after Michael Hyam, who was granted 41 acres of land there in 1859. Developer Henry Halloran, who bought up tracts of land around Jervis Bay following the declaration of the Jervis Bay Territory. was offering land for sale at Hyams Beach around 1916.

Landmarks:

  • Husky Ferry
  • Jervis Bay Kayak and Paddlesports Co
  • Booderee National Park
  • White sand and clear blue waters with enough waves to keep a young body boarder interested but calm enough for little kids entertained for hours
  • The water is clear and there are nice rocks to walk along at the beach's edge.
Lucky Bay Western Australia

History:

Matthew Flinders had sailed into the hazardous Archipelago of the Recherche, and found his ship surrounded by islands and rocks with nightfall coming on. He named this area "Lucky Bay" when his vessel HMS Investigator took refuge after a summer storm.

Landmarks:

In 1925, Brisbane hotelier Jim Cavill opened the Surfers Paradise Hotel located on what would later become the site of the Surfers Paradise Centre which incorporates the Surfers Paradise Beer Garden and Hard Rock Cafe. In opening the hotel and neighbouring zoo, Cavill created the first attraction in the suburb

  • The iconic skyline and golden beaches of Surfers Paradise is the Gold Coast's postcard image to Australia and the world.
